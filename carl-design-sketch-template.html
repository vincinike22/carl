Carl — Design Sketch Workbook

For rough thinking, not pretty drawing. Ugly sketches are welcome. The goal is to make your design intent visible enough that it can direct the machine.

How to use this

  • Draw fast. Do not try to make it beautiful.
  • Use arrows, boxes, labels, circles, strike-throughs, angry notes — whatever helps.
  • Focus on hierarchy, mood, emphasis, and product character.
  • If you hate something in the current prototype, write it bluntly.
  • If something should feel different, say exactly how.

What we are trying to discover

We want to know

  • What should dominate each screen?
  • What should recede or disappear?
  • What should feel private, exact, or investigative?
  • What makes Carl feel unlike a journaling or wellness app?

We do NOT need

  • pixel-perfect layouts
  • beautiful handwriting
  • a complete design system
  • final colors or fonts chosen perfectly

Quick design prompts

What do I hate? What should dominate? What should disappear? What should the user feel in 5 seconds? What makes this feel like Carl? What still feels generic?

Screen 1 — Home / Entry

This is the first 5–15 seconds. Not the full product. What is the first impression? What is the first action? What must the user feel immediately?

Sketch area
What I hate in the current version
What should dominate instead
What should disappear or move to background
What should the user feel in 5 seconds?
If this screen were a physical object/room, what would it be?

Screen 2 — Post-entry output

This is the core product moment. What appears after writing? What should feel sharp? What should feel provisional? What should feel evidence-linked?

Sketch area
What should the main result be?
What should not be the main result?
How should charge / contradiction / recurrence show up?
What makes this feel investigative rather than reflective?
What line or phrase absolutely should NOT appear here?

Screen 3 — Weekly review / case review

This should not feel like a mindful wrap-up. It should feel like reviewing evidence from the week. Sketch the hierarchy and write what should feel proven, unproven, weak, or worth watching.

Sketch area
What should this review feel like?
What should it absolutely not feel like?
How should evidence appear?
What should remain open / unproven?
If this screen had one job only, what would it be?
